## ARK INNOVATION FRAMEWORK

### Section 1 — Disruption Qualification Check
*Before proceeding, establish whether {{SYMBOL}} qualifies for this lens.*

**Is {{SYMBOL}} an innovation platform or a beneficiary of one?**
- **Innovator**: The company IS the technology (e.g., a payments platform,
  an AI-driven diagnostics company, an EV manufacturer)
- **Beneficiary**: The company uses technology to disrupt incumbents
  (e.g., a digital-first insurer using ML for underwriting)
- **Traditional with tech overlay**: Has an app but the core business model
  is unchanged — does NOT qualify for ARK lens

If {{SYMBOL}} falls into the third category, state: *"This company does not
exhibit the characteristics of disruptive innovation. Recommend re-analysing
under the Warren Buffett or Sequoia lens."*

If it qualifies, continue with the full framework below.

### Section 2 — S-Curve Positioning
ARK's core insight: disruptive technologies follow an S-curve of adoption.
The highest-return entry point is the early-to-mid phase, before mainstream
adoption and while the technology appears niche.

**Phase diagnosis for {{SYMBOL}}'s core technology:**
- **Phase 1 — Innovation Trigger**: Technology proven but not commercially
  viable. Losses acceptable. (0–2% market penetration)
- **Phase 2 — Early Adopters**: Product-market fit found. Revenue
  accelerating from low base. (2–15% penetration) — ARK's sweet spot
- **Phase 3 — Early Majority**: Mainstream adoption begins. Valuation
  expansion. (15–50% penetration)
- **Phase 4 — Late Majority**: Growth decelerates. Competition intensifies.
  Multiple compression risk. (50–80% penetration)

Estimate which phase {{SYMBOL}}'s primary market is in. Use
{{REVENUE_GROWTH_3YR}}% CAGR as a data point: true S-curve Phase 2 companies
show revenue growth accelerating, not just high.

### Section 3 — TAM (Total Addressable Market) Analysis
ARK believes most analysts dramatically under-estimate TAM because they
use current market data rather than projecting what the market becomes
as price deflates and adoption widens.

For {{SYMBOL}}'s India TAM:
- **Stated current TAM**: What is the conventional industry size estimate?
- **ARK-adjusted TAM**: If the technology achieves 50% cost reduction over
  5 years (Wright's Law), what new customer segments become addressable?
- **India demographic multiplier**: India has 1.4B people; even 5%
  penetration of a niche market = enormous absolute opportunity
- **Export TAM**: Is India a build-for-the-world platform (IT services model)
  or purely domestic? Global TAM changes the calculus entirely.

Revenue of {{REVENUE_GROWTH_3YR}}% CAGR: Is the company growing faster or
slower than its TAM? Slower than TAM = market share loss (bearish signal);
faster = share gain (key for early-stage disruptors).

### Section 4 — Wright's Law & Cost Deflation
ARK's analytical edge: they quantify the cost deflation curve.
*"As cumulative production doubles, costs fall by a fixed percentage."*

For {{SYMBOL}}:
- What is the primary cost input that is deflationary?
  (e.g., cloud computing costs, battery costs for EV players, DNA
  sequencing costs for genomics, digital marketing CPAs)
- Is the company's gross margin expanding as costs deflate while
  pricing holds? Operating margin {{OPERATING_MARGIN}}% — direction?
- What is the "magic price point" at which the technology becomes
  mass-market in India? (e.g., EVs at ₹8L, smartphones at ₹5K, etc.)
- How far is {{SYMBOL}}'s offering from that price point today?

### Section 5 — Platform Business Potential
ARK values platform businesses that host multiple products/services:

**Platform indicators for {{SYMBOL}}:**
- Does the company have a growing ecosystem of third-party participants
  (sellers, developers, partners, distributors)?
- Is there cross-sell / upsell data showing revenue per user expanding?
- Does the platform generate proprietary data that creates a compounding
  moat (more users → better model → better product → more users)?
- Network density: Is value creation accelerating with scale, or is
  this a linear business dressed as a platform?

### Section 6 — Valuation: ARK's Revenue Multiple Approach
ARK does not use PE ratios for early-stage disruptors (often unprofitable).
They use EV/Revenue multiples anchored to 5-year revenue scenarios.
